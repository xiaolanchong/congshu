Colloquial Slovene

1 Dobrodošli v Sloveniji Welcome to Slovenia

Prihod Arrival

BEN Dober dan! Oprostite, a ste vi gospa Mlakar? ALENKA Ja, sem. BEN Jaz sem Ben Pintar. ALENKA O, gospod Pintar, dobrodošli v Sloveniji! To je moj mož Andrej. ANDREJ Me veseli. ALENKA Gospod Pintar, dobro govorite slovensko. Ste Slovenec? BEN Ne, nisem, Američan sem. Moj oče je Slovenec.

BEN Hello! Excuse me, are you Mrs. Mlakar? ALENKA Yes, I am. BEN I'm Ben Pintar. ALENKA Oh, Mr. Pintar, welcome to Slovenia! This is my husband, Andrej. ANDREJ Nice to meet you. ALENKA Mr. Pintar, you speak Slovene well. Are you Slovene? BEN No, I am not. I am American. My father is Slovene.

Greeting people

dobro jutro ‘good morning' (until 8-9 am), dober dan 'hello' ('good day'), and dober večer 'good evening'. na svidenje (more casually adijo 'bye' lahko noč ‘good night'. živjo zdravo 'hi' and 'bye' on arrival and leaving, čao 'bye'

Jaz sem Ben Pintar. I'm Ben Pintar. A ste vi gospa Mlakar? Are you Mrs. Mlakar? Moj oče je Slovenec. My father is Slovene.

The auxiliary verb biti 'to be'

(jaz) sem I am (ti) si you are (on/ona/ono) je he/she/it is (midva/medve) sva we [two] are (vidva/vedve) sta you [two] are (onadva/onidve) sta they [two] are (mi/me) smo we are (vi/ve) ste you are (oni/one/ona) so they are

je -> ni 'is not'; other + ni- : nisem, nisi, nismo, . . . Nisem Slovenec. I am not Slovene.
